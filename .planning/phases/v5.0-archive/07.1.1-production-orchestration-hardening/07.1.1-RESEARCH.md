# Phase 7.1.1 Research

## Summary
Recent multi-agent orchestration systems emphasize durable state, idempotent actions, and explicit queueing/concurrency controls. The clearest signals are Git-backed orchestration systems (Beads, Gas Town) and production-oriented agent platforms that treat workflows as replayable graphs with checkpoints.

## Reference Signals
- Gas Town overview and workflow model (Mayor/Convoy/Agent) for durable multi-agent coordination:
  - https://www.wal.sh/research/gastown.html
  - https://github.com/steveyegge/gastown
- Beads: git-backed graph issue tracker for AI agents with dependency-aware execution:

  - https://medium.com/@lorenzozar/use-git-worktree-to-run-multiple-claude-code-agents-a1d47ef972d5
  - https://incident.io/blog/shipping-faster-with-claude-code-and-git-worktrees

## Applied Takeaways
- Durable, replayable state is the foundation of production reliability.
- Parallel agents should operate in isolated worktrees to reduce merge conflicts.
- Orchestrator should enforce idempotency to protect against tool retries.
- Failure taxonomies and recovery playbooks are required to reduce human intervention.
