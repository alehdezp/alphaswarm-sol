# Research Plan: The Content Machine

**Main Question:** How to build a semi-automated, creative content generation system that turns daily project work (cybersecurity research, Claude Code automation, Solidity/Rust learning, own projects) into publishable content — with human refinement as the final step, integrated simply into MSD phase workflows.

**Key Requirements:**
- NOT fully automatic — produces drafts for human editing and personalization
- Creative, personalized format (not corporate/generic)
- Covers multiple domains: cybersecurity, Claude Code, Solidity, Rust, general tech
- Integrates with MSD (phase management) without bloating the workflow
- Can generate content from phase work, feature development, research, experiments
- Outputs: LinkedIn posts, blog articles, threads, technical deep-dives, learning logs

---

## Subtopic 1: Developer Content Machines & Personal Knowledge Systems
**Agent focus:** How do prolific developer-writers build content pipelines from their daily work? What systems exist (digital gardens, second brains, zettelkasten, build-in-public)? How do they go from "I learned something" to "published post"? Focus on developers who write about cybersecurity, web3, systems programming, AI tooling. What makes their content feel authentic vs generic?

**Expected output:** Patterns, systems, tools, examples of developer-writers who successfully turn project work into content. Anti-patterns (what makes content feel robotic/corporate).

## Subtopic 2: AI-Assisted Content Creation Frameworks
**Agent focus:** What LLM-powered tools and frameworks exist for semi-automated content creation? How do people use Claude/GPT to draft content while maintaining their voice? What are the best prompting patterns for content that doesn't feel AI-generated? Focus on technical writing specifically. Include any Claude Code skills, MCP servers, or agentic workflows for content.

**Expected output:** Tools, prompting strategies, voice-preservation techniques, specific frameworks for AI-assisted technical writing.

## Subtopic 3: Technical Content Formats & Creative Differentiation
**Agent focus:** What content formats work best for technical topics (cybersecurity findings, smart contract analysis, Rust/Solidity learning, tool automation)? How do standout technical creators differentiate? What are the most engaging formats on LinkedIn vs blogs vs newsletters? Focus on formats that are creative and personalized, not dry tutorials. Include: learning-in-public logs, experiment reports, "what I tried and failed" posts, contrarian takes, metric reveals, teardown analysis.

**Expected output:** Format catalog with engagement data, creative differentiation strategies, examples from top technical creators, platform-specific format recommendations.

## Subtopic 4: Knowledge-to-Content Extraction Patterns
**Agent focus:** How to systematically extract publishable content from project artifacts? This includes: decision logs (ADRs), research notes, experiment results, code reviews, debugging sessions, architecture decisions, failed approaches. What metadata/structure makes documents "content-ready"? How do organizations like Stripe, Cloudflare, Trail of Bits turn internal research into public blog posts? Focus on patterns that work at the individual/small-team level.

**Expected output:** Extraction patterns, document structures that enable content mining, examples from companies that publish from internal work, lightweight metadata schemas.

## Subtopic 5: Lightweight Dev-Workflow Integration
**Agent focus:** How to embed content generation into development workflows WITHOUT adding friction? Focus on: git hooks, CI triggers, session summaries, planning-tool integration, "content seeds" captured during work. How do people integrate content into project management (like MSD phases) simply? What's the minimum viable integration that produces maximum output? Anti-pattern: systems that require separate "content time" disconnected from building.

**Expected output:** Integration patterns, minimum-friction approaches, examples of content captured as byproduct of work, simple workflow modifications that yield content.

---

## Synthesis Plan
Combine all findings into a comprehensive report that answers:
1. What system architecture to build (tools + workflow + formats)
2. How to integrate it with MSD phase management
3. What content formats to prioritize per domain (cybersecurity, Solidity, Rust, Claude Code)
4. What skills/agents to create
5. A phased implementation roadmap
